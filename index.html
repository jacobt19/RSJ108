How to cover a college basketball game

time: 2-3 hours
awesomeness rating: 100%

College basketball games are one of the most exciting sports events. This will help you book a journalism gig and cover the event

  • Things You'll Need

  • event pass
  • digital camera
  • laptop with editing software

  1. Aquire an event pass from a coach, team manager, or other person involved with the team.

    This can be done in a number of ways. Sending an email, messaging on social media, or visiting their office to speak in person are all viable options

  2. Buy a digital camera with a memory drive if you don't have one already.

    You don't have to spend a huge amount of money for this. Buy the cheapest camera at Bestbuy for $150 if you want. Similarly, free editing software such as Shotcut can be used in place of Adobe Premier. Regardless, a quality camera and decent editing software is a must for this kind of work.

    Good preparation is the differnce between pro and amateur work.

  3. Position yourself in a good spot to get good camera angles.

    Down by the baseline (the top ends of the court) would be the most obvious choice.The student section may also provide good angles. If the team allows you to be near their bench, that would be great. You can switch between the positions to get a variety of shots. The video below has great advice for planning, filming, and editing sports events.

  4. Film!

    Take photos and film footage of the game. Get shots of the players doing warm-up drills and the dance routine at halftime. When the game is done, the student section might rush the floor if it was a close win. This could be a great chance to get an iconic photo. Keep your eyes and ears open, and have fun.

  5. Take out the memory disc from the camera and stick into your laptop.

    Take out the memory disc from the camera and stick into your laptop. Open the editing software and create a masterpiece of a video. Or, just touch up the photos if that's all the gig calls for.
